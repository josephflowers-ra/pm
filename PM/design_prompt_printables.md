# Design Prompt — Project Management Guide Printables

## Project

I'm designing a set of printable one-pagers to accompany a book called **Project Management Made Simple: A Beginner's Guide to Getting Things Done**. The printables should feel like a matched set — a workbook you'd tear pages out of — not a random pile of templates.

## Audience

First-time or reluctant project managers. People who didn't seek out the role but got asked to run something. They're smart but busy. They won't read a dense manual. They want a sheet they can fill in on their laptop or print and scribble on.

## Tone and visual feel

- Warm, calm, practical. Clear hierarchy. Generous white space.
- Dry humor is fine; corporate-speak is not.
- One accent color — that's plenty. Works in B&W if printed.
- Friendly-but-serious sans-serif (think Inter, Söhne, or Source Sans). Clear type scale.
- No stock photos. No "business people in a meeting" imagery. No clip art.
- Looks equally good on screen and on plain 8.5x11" paper.
- Section headers in a consistent style across every sheet.

## Format

- Letter (8.5" x 11") or A4 — your call, but pick one and stick with it.
- One page per printable unless noted.
- Fillable blanks: light gray rules, checkboxes, or thin-bordered text cells.
- A consistent header and footer on every sheet (title, small page label, subtle brand mark — not a giant logo).

## Running example

The book threads a **Community Cleanup Day** project through every chapter (100+ volunteers, three parks, March 28 event, $1,000 budget). Feel free to use it in any demo copy, but the printables themselves should be blank and reusable.

## Printables to design

1. **Project Charter** (one page)
   Sections: Project Name, Purpose, Sponsor, Project Lead, Key Stakeholders, Outcome / Success Criteria, In Scope, Out of Scope, High-Level Timeline, Budget, Top Risks, How We Decide. Signature block at the bottom.

2. **Weekly Status Update** (one page)
   Status indicator at top (Green / Amber / Red — three circles to check, with a one-line reason). Then: Progress this period · Risks & Blockers · Decisions Needed (with a deadline field) · What's Next. Should be fillable in under 10 minutes.

3. **Risk Matrix** (one page)
   3x3 grid at the top (Low/Med/High probability × Low/Med/High impact) with cells subtly shaded for priority. Table below with: Risk · Probability · Impact · Response Plan · Owner. 6–8 empty rows.

4. **Lessons Learned** (one page)
   Four sections: What went well · What did not go well · What to improve next time · Actions (with owner and due date).

5. **RACI Chart** (one page)
   Task column plus R / A / C / I columns. 10–12 empty rows. Short legend at the top (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed).

6. **Chapter Cheat Sheets** (six one-pagers, matched set)
   Each uses the same template: **Big ideas · Do this · Avoid this · Try it.** These are reference cards, not fill-ins. I'll provide the content per chapter (1: Goals & Scope, 2: Tasks & Tools, 3: Timelines, 4: Communication, 5: Troubleshooting, 6: Closing). Design task is the consistent card style.

## Deliverables I want from you

- A small visual system: color, type, header style, fill-in-blank convention. I want to be able to add a seventh or eighth printable later and have it look like it belongs.
- Each printable as a standalone PDF-ready page.
- A cover sheet / index that lists all the printables in the set.

## What I do NOT want

- Stock imagery, cartoon illustrations, or generic "business" art.
- Dense layouts that look like tax forms or insurance paperwork.
- Three-column layouts that slice the content visually without a reason.
- Heavy borders, drop shadows, or 2010-era skeuomorphism.

## Optional stretch

If you have capacity: a small page-numbered "divider card" for each chapter that matches the cheat sheet style, so the full pack feels like a deliberate workbook.
